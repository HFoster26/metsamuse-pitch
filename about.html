The Full Story

The Finnish Forest Museum Lusto celebrates the enduring bond between people and forests, showcasing how deeply they shape Finnish life and identity. While the museum preserves this heritage within its walls, it still lacks direct engagement with the living forests it represents.

The Mets√§Muse Project bridges this gap by extending the museum experience just two kilometers away into the Punkaharju research forests, managed by the Natural Resources Institute of Finland (Luke). These forests, home to more than one hundred tree species, are living archives of biodiversity, climate research, and cultural memory.

Through the Mets√§Muse mobile app, visitors can explore trails, listen to forest stories, and experience how research meets tradition. By connecting digital storytelling with ecological understanding, Mets√§Muse strengthens Lusto's mission and supports its goal of achieving UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage recognition.
